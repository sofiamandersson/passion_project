Knowledge - Feedback Staircase

The feedback staircase is a good tool to have in the back of your mind

2 Why and Background

When giving feedback it’s important to base it of past common experiences, like the philosopher Søren Kierkegaard said ‘Life can only be understood backwards; but it must be lived forwards’. So let’s go back in time with the intention to understand it.

  1. Gather everyone around the empty wall or whiteboard
  2. Create a timeline for the past 3 months
  3. Together pinpoint the major events during this period, like; deadlines, project deliveries, team-days etc
  4. Write them on post-its and place these events on the timeline
  5. Now take 10 minutes for individual reflection, ask yourself what’s been good and what could have been better during these events.
    It could be things like:
    • team dynamics
    • your own and others behaviors
    • the stress level of the team etc.
  6. Write one reflections on one post-it, creating one pile of good and another of bad ones
  7. One by one go up to the wall and read out what you’ve written on the post-it Starting with the good ones and then place them above the timeline. Do the same thing for the bad ones but place them under the timeline.
  8. When everyone has put up their post-its it’s time for collective reflections, have an open discussion with the purpose of clarifying and understand each others perspectives.
  9. Together agree on five learnings for the future
  10. Decide for one person write them down and add them in the window bellow

3 Feedback time - The Hand

Time for the official feedback exercise - The Hand. The exercise is divided into five different steps:

Thumb up A behaviour you like about the person

Point at Something you want to point out, make the person aware of

F**k u A behaviour you don't like in the person

To develop Something that you want to see more of in the person

That little extra Something that you appreciate about the person

  1. Get some paper and pens
  2. Take 20 minutes to reflect and write some bullet points for each of the five steps - one paper for one person
  3. When everyone’s done it’s time to share the feedback
  4. Decide for one person that will start receiving the feedbac
  5. Then the rest of the team, one by one, read out the feedback you have for this person and give the paper to them
  6. When this person has received feedback from everyone you do the same for the next person
  7. Continue until everyone has received and given feedback to everyone in the team

4 Reflections and Insights

Now it’s time to round this session off. You have both received and given feedback to all team members, and it’s likely that some unexpected feelings and events took place, which is great but it’s important not to leave anything you've opened unfinished. For your own sake as well as the teams.

  1. Take 10 minutes for individual reflection and write down your main insights from what has happened during the session
  2. Take out you phone/computer and go to menti.com
  3. Enter the code bellow and follow the instructions
  4. When everyones done gather the team sitting in a circle and click 'show results'
  5. One by one read out your reply when its shown in the window bellow make sure to ask if you dont understand
  6. Go full circle until everyone has answered
